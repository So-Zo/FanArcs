The Basics

"Anime," also known as "Animation" in its origin country of Japan. In Japan, anime refers to all animated works, both drawn and computer-generated. In places outside of Japan, especially in the West, "anime" refers to Japanese-inspired animation, while "animation" tends to be used for all other works of animated entertainment. It is necessary to know that not all anime is Japanese, but it is generally Japanese-inspired or based. South Korea is also notable for its contributions as well. See the page titled "Manga" for more information.


Based on what we know, the earliest known works of anime are dated 1917 and, the wideapread designs and characteristics of anime were created in the 1960s and attributed to a man named Osamu Tezuka

Anime Overview

Anime has a massive catalog of subgeneres and types of stories to choose. to make it easy for you here are all the most noteable ones.


  • Action: Focuses on intense sequences of physical activity, combat, or conflict. Often features battles or high-energy scenes.
  • Adventure: Involves characters traveling or exploring new worlds, facing challenges, and discovering new places or people.
  • Comedy: Primarily designed to make the audience laugh, often through exaggerated situations, humorous dialogue, and character antics.
  • Drama: Focuses on realistic storytelling and emotional character development, exploring deep themes like relationships, loss, and personal growth.
  • Fantasy: Set in fantastical worlds with elements like magic, mythical creatures, and supernatural powers, often featuring epic quests.
  • Horror: Aimed at creating fear and suspense, often involving supernatural beings, monsters, or psychological horror elements.
  • Mystery: Centers on solving a puzzle or crime, usually with a detective or investigator character working to uncover the truth.
  • Romance: Focuses on romantic relationships between characters, exploring love, relationships, and emotional connections.
  • Sci-Fi: Explores futuristic concepts, technology, space travel, and speculative ideas often grounded in scientific possibilities.
  • Slice of Life: Depicts the everyday experiences of characters, typically focusing on their personal lives, relationships, and daily routines.
  • Sports: Centers around competitive sports and athletes, often focusing on teamwork, training, and personal growth through competition.
  • Super Natrual: Involves paranormal events, creatures, or powers, often set in the real world but with otherworldly elements affecting the characters‚Äô lives.
  • Thriller: Builds tension and excitement, often with high stakes, mystery, and a focus on psychological conflict or danger.
  • Mecha: Features large robots, typically piloted by humans, often set in futuristic or dystopian settings.
  • Shounen: Aimed at young male audiences, featuring action, adventure, and strong male protagonists overcoming obstacles.
  • Shoujo: Aimed at young female audiences, often focusing on romance, relationships, and emotional growth of female protagonists.
  • Seinen: Targeted at adult men, with more complex themes, often darker or more mature, exploring deeper societal and personal issues.
  • Josei: Targeted at adult women, often featuring realistic romance and relationships with a more mature, realistic tone.
